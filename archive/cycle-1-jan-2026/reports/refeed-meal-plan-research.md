# 7-Day Refeed Meal Plan: Evidence-Based Protocol After Extended Fasting

*Date: 2026-01-12 | Sources: 12+*

## Summary

- **Days 1-2 (500-800 cal):** Bone broth and eggs are ideal first foods - gentle, protein-rich, minimal insulin spike
- **Days 3-5 (1200-1500 cal):** 120-130g protein is appropriate for muscle rebuilding with mTOR now active
- **Days 6-7 (1800-2000 cal):** Safe to reintroduce complex carbs after electrolyte restoration
- **Key safety measures:** Continue electrolytes, eat slowly, stop at 80% full, scale back if bloating occurs
- **Foods to avoid initially:** High-carb foods, processed foods, dairy (for many), raw vegetables, alcohol

---

## Why This Protocol? The Clinical Basis

### NICE Guidelines Context

Clinical guidelines for refeeding after starvation recommend:

- Start at **50% of energy requirements** for those who haven't eaten for >5 days [1]
- For severely malnourished: Start at **5 kcal/kg/day** (~350-400 cal for 70kg person) [1]
- Increase calories by 5-10 kcal/kg/day if no refeeding symptoms [1]

**The infographic's 500-600 cal Day 1** is conservative and appropriate for healthy individuals after 10-11 day fast.

## Day-by-Day Analysis

### Day 1: 500-600 cal

**Breakfast: Bone broth**
**Lunch: Bone broth**
**Dinner: 2 soft scrambled eggs**

#### Why Bone Broth First?

"Bone broth includes amino acids (glutamine, glycine, proline, histidine, arginine) and minerals (Ca, P, K, Mg, Zn) that support the **enhancement of gut health**, alleviate inflammation in the intestinal barrier, and improve intestinal barrier function" [2].

Key benefits:
1. **Electrolytes** - Natural source of sodium, potassium, magnesium
2. **Glycine** - "Helps generate collagen, boasts anti-inflammatory properties to aid in gut repair, promotes healthy gut flora" [3]
3. **Glutamine** - Supports intestinal cell regeneration
4. **Collagen/gelatin** - Gentle on digestive system
5. **Liquid form** - Easy to digest with reduced enzyme production

#### Why Eggs?

- High-quality, complete protein
- Minimal carbohydrates (no insulin spike)
- Easy to digest when soft-scrambled
- Rich in choline for liver health
- Contains B vitamins

**Verdict:** Day 1 protocol is well-designed and evidence-based.

---

### Day 2: 700-800 cal

**Breakfast: Broth + 1 egg**
**Lunch: 4oz salmon, avocado**
**Dinner: 2 eggs, spinach**

#### Progression Rationale

- Small calorie increase (30-40%)
- Introduction of fatty fish (omega-3s, protein)
- Addition of vegetables (spinach - low fiber, nutrient-dense)
- Avocado provides healthy fats and potassium

Still no significant carbohydrates - managing insulin sensitivity.

---

### Day 3: 1200 cal

**Breakfast: 3 eggs, avocado**
**Lunch: 6oz chicken, zucchini**
**Dinner: 5oz salmon, asparagus**

#### Key Change: Protein Ramp-Up

By Day 3, the focus shifts to protein:
- ~120-130g protein target begins
- mTOR is reactivating and ready for protein synthesis
- Muscle protein synthesis can now proceed effectively

#### Still No Significant Carbs

Continuing low-carb because:
- Insulin sensitivity remains elevated
- Refeeding syndrome risk decreases but isn't eliminated
- Body is still adapting to food intake

---

### Days 4-5: 1400-1600 cal

Continued protein emphasis with variety:
- Various protein sources (beef, fish, chicken)
- Non-starchy vegetables
- Healthy fats

This is the **muscle rebuilding phase** where adequate protein is critical.

---

### Days 6-7: 1800-2000 cal

**Carbohydrate Reintroduction:**
- Day 6: Rice introduced with salmon
- Day 7: Sweet potato, potato hash

#### Why Carbs Are Safe Now

1. Electrolytes have been replenished over 5 days
2. Insulin sensitivity is normalizing
3. Gut has had time to restore enzyme production
4. Body has adapted to food intake

#### Choice of Carbs

**Complex carbohydrates chosen:**
- Sweet potato - nutrient-dense, moderate glycemic
- Rice - easily digestible
- Regular potato - provides potassium

**Avoiding:**
- Refined carbs
- Sugars
- High-fiber raw foods (too hard to digest initially)

## Daily Targets Analysis

### Protein: 120-130g

This target is appropriate for:
- **Muscle rebuilding** after potential losses during fast
- **mTOR activation** for protein synthesis
- Standard recommendation of 1.6-2.2g/kg for muscle building

"High protein protects metabolism" - the infographic's key principle is supported by protein's role in:
- Maintaining/rebuilding lean mass
- Supporting metabolic rate
- Providing amino acids for tissue repair

### Water: 2-3L

Essential because:
- Refeeding increases metabolic demands
- Supports kidney function as metabolites clear
- Prevents constipation as food intake resumes

### Continue Electrolytes

Critical in early refeed:
- NICE recommends: K+ 2-4 mmol/kg/day, PO4 0.3-0.6 mmol/kg/day, Mg 0.4 mmol/kg/day [1]
- Even with food, supplementation may be needed
- Monitor for signs of deficiency (fatigue, muscle cramps, irregular heartbeat)

### Avoid Processed Foods

Sound advice because:
- High sodium can cause water retention
- Refined carbs spike insulin
- Additives may irritate sensitive gut
- Empty calories when nutrition density matters most

---

## Bottom Tips Analysis

### "Eat slowly, chew thoroughly"

**Evidence-based:**
- Digestive enzyme production is reduced after extended fasting
- Slow eating improves digestion
- Allows satiety signals to register
- Reduces risk of digestive distress

### "Stop at 80% full"

**Appropriate because:**
- Stomach capacity may be reduced
- Prevents overeating when hunger signals are dysregulated
- Traditional wisdom (Okinawan "hara hachi bu")
- Reduces digestive burden

### "Scale back if bloating"

**Critical safety advice:**
- Bloating may indicate:
  - Too rapid calorie advancement
  - Food intolerance
  - Digestive enzyme insufficiency
- Reducing intake and slowing progression is appropriate response

### "Prioritize protein"

**Well-supported:**
- Protein drives mTOR-mediated rebuilding
- Supports lean mass preservation
- Has highest thermic effect (supporting metabolism)
- Most satiating macronutrient

## What's Missing: Additional Recommendations

Based on research, consider adding:

### 1. Probiotics/Fermented Foods (Day 3+)

Gut microbiome shifts during fasting; fermented foods support restoration:
- Small amounts of sauerkraut, kimchi, or kefir
- Introduce gradually

### 2. Digestive Enzyme Support

"Digestive enzymes are reduced during fasting" [4]. Consider:
- Chewing thoroughly (activates salivary enzymes)
- Digestive enzyme supplement with meals
- Apple cider vinegar before meals (stimulates stomach acid)

### 3. Specific Foods to AVOID in First 48 Hours

Not explicitly stated in infographic:
- **Alcohol** - liver is recovering
- **Dairy** - many develop temporary lactose intolerance
- **Raw vegetables** - too fibrous
- **Nuts and seeds** - difficult to digest
- **Spicy foods** - may irritate sensitive gut
- **High-FODMAP foods** - may cause bloating
- **Caffeine** - can stress adrenals
